The System

What is Double Monkey Trading?

DMT is a thinking framework disguised as a trading system. Easy enough for a five-year-old to follow — like a game. Serious enough to fund your future.

01
Double Confirmation System

Every trade requires confirmation before entry. The best DMT trades have both systems — 3 Monkey Jump AND Double Liquidity — present at the same time.

02
Think in Bets — Always

Every trade is a calculated bet with positive expected value. The motto is non-negotiable. No trade is placed without a clear edge and defined risk-reward.

03
Channel Break First

No channel break = no trade. Period. DMT begins when price breaks the track channel — then the system activates. No break, no action. Ever.

04
Liquidity is the Target

Price hunts liquidity — pools of stop losses clustered at key levels. DMT counts these sweeps as jumps. LQ3 is always the entry zone.

05
Big Win. Small Loss.

Stop loss = 1 to 2 candles below entry. Take profit = minimum 3x the stop. That is the law. One trade following this law beats 20 average trades.

06
Calm, Structured, Funded

0.25% risk per trade. Two wins and you leave. No chasing. No overtrading. The system gives you calmness — not anxiety. Built for funded accounts.

Do I need trading experience?
No. Version 1 starts from absolute zero — what Forex is, pips, candles, channels. If you already have experience, skip straight to Version 2 and apply DMT to what you already know.
Can I use DMT with ICT or Al Brooks?
Yes — that is by design. DMT does not replace your existing education. It organises it and adds a big win framework. Mix it in, tweak your edge, and the results grow.
Which markets can I trade with DMT?
Any market with a chart. Forex, Gold, Stocks, Crypto, Indices, Commodities. If it has price moving between highs and lows — DMT works. Professor primarily trades Gold.
How many trades per week?
One to two quality trades per week is the target. After two wins — close your charts. Quality over quantity. This discipline is what creates a consistent weekly edge.
What timeframe should I start on?
The 5 Minute chart is the recommended starting point. Professor Iftekhar uses the 5M as his primary chart. Once you master it there, the logic transfers to every timeframe automatically.
What is the minimum risk per trade?
Always 0.25% of your account per trade. This is the funded trader standard. Stop loss = 1 to 2 candles below entry. Take profit = minimum 3x that stop. Small, defined, logical.
